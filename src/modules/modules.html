Modules

Each individual week of this semester was coupled into one of the four total modules that this class followed as directives for success. At the end every module there was a comprehensive exam over that module which included a range of assignments and book readings. You may consider the following as "homeworks," but I believe they served greater purpose than just an assignment. Between the readings from the text, and the online videos from Professor Messer and Eli the Computer Guy, these modules served as week long study guides, in which concepts provided by the text was made clearer by the videos that were assigned to watch.

Module 1 - ISO/OSI Model

Module one served as a foundation that really set the tone for what to expect in this class, specifically the ISO/OSI model, and the physical media that interacts with a network infrastructure. I placed a lot of focus on the protocols that are handled within the OSI Modela and the type of media that it used to interact from layer to layer.

Bellow you will find two assignments that I enjoyed completing. I have found that taking notes whilst reading or watching videos helps me understand the information and the concepts. Many times I'd answer the questions without having to consult my notes or any other resource. Enjoy!

Module 2 - TCP/IP & Switches

Module two we start breaking down the OSI model into pieces, focusing more on individual layers, and the types of protocols concerning those layers. For this module we focused on the Data Link Layer, the Network Layer, and the Transport Layer. I placed a lot of focus on this assignment because I felt it was one of the more important subject of the module. I recall getting this test being my highest grade.

Module three focused on how data communication moved from one network/subnet/physical structure to another with the use of backbones, different broadband connection types, DNS, and a framework for network security. Module 4 - Cloud Services && The IoT!

Module four was my favorite module because it shed light on how wireless networking is made possible. At a fundamental level, I knew that some sort of wave was sent from antenna to antenna. However, I learned that it goes deeper, that it starts at an alternating current that moves 1's and 0's to an antenna that produces radio frequencies that move within the electromagnetic spectrum. Simple, right? It discusses the different agencies and their branches that govern the space and time which radio frequencies can travel.

Furthermore, it introduces a focus on Cloud Computing and the Internet of Things. This seems appropriate because it demonstrates how everything we've learned up to this point in class is all tying to a new level of infrastructure that is being adopted as a standard.
